Laundromat Business Analysis

How do you track the health of a brick and mortar business when there's no pre-existing data infrastructure? To simulate real-world operations, I built an end-to-end data pipeline for a 22-asset laundromat (washers, dryers, and vending machines) entirely from scratch. This project bridges the gap between raw backend engineering and decision making by transforming synthetic transaction data into actionable financial and operational insights.

  • Launch Live Dashboard
Phase 1: Relational Data Design

Database Architecture
& Python Pipeline

Before you can visualize insights, you need a realistic, high-integrity data warehouse. Using Python (Pandas & NumPy), I created a data generation engine to model a four-table relational database spanning multiple years. Instead of generating random numbers, I programmed complex, real business logic into the pipeline—including a realistic consumer grand-opening ramp curve, weekend demand spikes, and strict financial variances for utility and inventory costs (COGS) to mirror genuine operational expenses.

Phase 2: Data Visualization & Strategy

Business Health Metrics
& Trend Analysis

Data is only as valuable as the decisions it enables. I translated the backend data architecture into an interactive Tableau dashboard designed to monitor revenue velocity and operational trends. The final build isolates performance across product categories (Washers vs. Dryers vs. Vending Machines), calculates true year-over-year profit margins on a $250K+ revenue portfolio, and visually captures the exact point where the business successfully matured past its initial operational ramp-up phase.
